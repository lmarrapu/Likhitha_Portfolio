Decoding Cellular Complexity: A Deep Learning Expedition in Blood Cell Image Classification

Authors
Likhitha Marrapu, Shivani Battu, Chandini Karrothu, Anuranjani Thota
Conference
Future of Information and Communication Conference (FICC) 2025
Location
Berlin, Germany
Date
April, 2025
Abstract
Our research introduces an innovative deep learning framework for blood cell image classification, establishing new benchmarks in medical diagnostics accuracy. By developing a hybrid architecture combining InceptionV3 and Xception models, we achieved an exceptional 98.51% test accuracy on a comprehensive dataset of 17,092 expertly-annotated blood cell images from the Hospital Clinic of Barcelona. Through rigorous preprocessing techniques including standardized image resizing and class imbalance correction via SMOTE, we established a robust foundation for model training. Our comparative analysis across multiple architectures—including CNNs, U-Net, ResNet-50, VGG-16, DenseNet-201, MobileNet-V2, and Supervised Contrastive Learning—demonstrated the superior performance of our hybrid approach. This research offers significant potential for early disease detection, including leukemia and autoimmune disorders, while providing valuable insights into model generalization through extensive data shuffling experiments and cross-validation protocols.
